# PickleCrew Product Context

## Why This Project Exists
Recreational pickleball players lack a dedicated platform for managing their community experience. They resort to:
- Group chats that become chaotic and hard to search
- Spreadsheets for tracking scores and stats
- Manual scorekeeping with no automated analytics
- No centralized way to find players or organize games

PickleCrew solves these problems by providing a purpose-built, mobile-first platform.

## How It Should Work

### Core User Flows
1. **Log a Game** — Quick score entry, select opponents, automatic ELO calculation
2. **Join/Manage a Crew** — Create or join a group of players, see crew stats
3. **Find Players** — Discover nearby players, see availability
4. **Challenge/Rivalry** — Send challenges, track rivalry records, hot streaks
5. **View Leaderboard** — See rankings by ELO, win rate, streaks
6. **Earn Badges** — Achievement system for milestones (Iron Dinker, Golden Pickle, etc.)

### User Experience Goals
- **Mobile-first** — Primary experience is on mobile; responsive to desktop
- **Friendly competitor energy** — Encouraging but with edge, like your most enthusiastic pickleball buddy
- **Concise and action-first** — Short imperative CTAs: "Log a Game", "Join Crew", "Challenge Sent"
- **Community-coded language** — "Crew", "Smashers", "Rivals" — names that feel earned and social
- **Celebratory** — Badges, streaks, Golden Pickle get spotlight moments
- **Never corporate** — Active voice, no jargon

### Design Philosophy
- Clean, sporty, modern aesthetic
- Navy + Blue + Fire color palette conveys competitive energy
- Large score/stat displays for impact
- Subtle animations for delight (badge pop-ins, score count-ups)
- No heavy parallax or excessive gradients
